# Dunford Sales Pitch: Detailed Component Examples

## Component 1: The Insight — Example

```
Insight for [Sales Intelligence Platform]:

Market Truth: "Sales teams are drowning in contact data but starving for actual buying signals."

Why this matters:
- Most sales tools focus on quantity of contacts (emails, phone numbers)
- But conversion rates drop when reps can't identify who's actually in-market
- Buyers have gotten better at ignoring generic outreach
- The real competitive advantage is knowing WHO to contact and WHEN

Customers who understand this insight value our differentiated approach to behavioral signals over static contact databases. Those who don't understand it just see us as "another contact database."

This insight sets up why our alternative analysis and "perfect world" will differ from traditional sales intelligence vendors.
```

## Component 2: Alternatives — Example

```
Alternatives Analysis for [Sales Intelligence Platform]:

Alternative 1: Traditional Contact Databases (ZoomInfo, Apollo)
Best for: Teams that need volume of contacts for broad outreach
Pros:
- Comprehensive contact coverage
- Easy to export to CRM/sequences
- Familiar category with clear ROI model
Cons:
- No buying signal data (you're guessing who to contact)
- High cost for data that goes unused
- Doesn't solve the "who's in-market" problem from our insight

Alternative 2: DIY Research + LinkedIn Sales Navigator
Best for: Small teams with time to do manual research
Pros:
- Low cost (just labor + LinkedIn subscription)
- Flexible, can research exactly what you need
- Good for highly targeted account lists
Cons:
- Doesn't scale beyond 50-100 accounts
- No behavioral signals (still guessing timing)
- Sales time spent researching instead of selling

Alternative 3: Do Nothing (Use Inbound Leads Only)
Best for: Companies with strong inbound motion
Pros:
- No additional tooling cost
- Inbound leads are warmer
- Simple process
Cons:
- Limited TAM coverage
- Can't accelerate pipeline when growth stalls
- Competitors may be reaching your ideal customers first

Each alternative addresses part of the problem but has trade-offs. The question is: which trade-offs match your situation?
```

## Component 3: The Perfect World — Example

```
The Perfect World for [Sales Intelligence Platform]:

Given the insight (need buying signals not just contacts) and the alternatives (volume vs. manual vs. inbound-only), the ideal solution would:

1. **Identify In-Market Accounts**
- Surface companies showing active buying behavior
- Not just contacts, but signals of intent
- Automated, not manual research

2. **Prioritize Who to Contact and When**
- Focus sales effort on accounts most likely to convert
- Timing intelligence (reach out when they're researching)
- Quality over quantity of outreach

3. **Integrate Behavioral Data with Contact Data**
- Don't choose between signals and contacts
- Combine "who's in-market" with "who to contact"
- Single platform, not stitching together multiple tools

4. **Scale Without Linear Cost**
- Not priced per contact seat (or you're back to the volume model)
- Cover broad TAM without breaking budget
- Accessible to teams of any size

This perfect world eliminates the core trade-offs: you get buying signals AND contacts, automation AND accuracy, scale AND affordability.
```

## Component 7: Objections — Example

```
Proactive Objection Handling for [Sales Intelligence Platform]:

Objection 1: "We already have ZoomInfo - why add another tool?"
Response: "Most of our customers keep their contact database. We're not replacing it - we're adding the 'when' layer. ZoomInfo tells you WHO exists; we tell you who's in-market RIGHT NOW. They're complementary. In fact, 70% of our customers integrate both."

Objection 2: "What if your intent data is wrong?"
Response: "Great question. Our intent score is based on 15+ behavioral signals, not just one data point. And we show you the underlying signals - 'visited these pages, downloaded this content, searched these terms' - so reps can judge for themselves. We've also back-tested: accounts with 70+ intent score are 8x more likely to close than cold outreach. Not perfect, but dramatically better than guessing."

Objection 3: "Our team is already overwhelmed with tools"
Response: "We actually reduce tools for most customers. You're likely using LinkedIn Sales Nav for research, manual account lists in spreadsheets, and maybe another intent tool. We consolidate that into a single workflow that feeds directly into your CRM. Most teams find this simplifies their process."
```
